Tiramisu

Recipe courtesy of Hong Nguyen, 2010

3 egg yolks

2 of 17.6 oz container mascarpone (Galbani – Italy product)

1 pint heavy cream, whipped

1 cup of sugar

5-6 shots of cooled espresso

¾ cup of Amaretto (Disaronno)

2 tablespoon of sugar for the espresso and liqueur

ladyfingers

cacao powder

 

Using an electric mixer, whip yolks and sugar until thick and pale in color (ribbon stage). Add mascarpone and whip until well incorporated. In a separate bowl, whip heavy cream, gradually add sugar, to a stiff glossy peak. Mix the cream into mascarpone and yolk mixture.

 

In a separate bowl with espresso, dip 1 ladyfinger at a time in the espresso mixture (only dip one side). In a 9 by 13-inch glass dish, arrange dipped ladyfingers close together in neat rows until the bottom of the dish is completely covered. Pour almost 1/2 of the mousse over the ladyfingers and smooth with a rubber spatula. Create another layer of ladyfingers and mousse. Top that layer with a layer of lady fingers and a very light layer of mouse (like frosting).

 

Chill until set (overnight). Sprinkle cacao powder on top right before serving.

 

The key to this recipe is not to skimp on the ingredients. When possible buy Italian products for the ladyfingers and marscapone.

 

Yields 4 servings
