Black Bean Chili

Recipe courtesy of Karla Palmer, 2013

1 pound dried black beans (2 1/2 cups), rinsed

1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil

¼ cup mustard seeds

2 tablespoons New Mexican chili powder

1½ teaspoons cumin seeds, or ground cumin

½ teaspoon cardamom seeds, or ground cardamom

2 medium onions, coarsely chopped

2-3 Cups dried oyster mushrooms, chopped

8 ounces tomatillos, husked, rinsed and coarsely chopped

¼ cup water

5½ cups vegetable or mushroom broth

1 6-ounce can tomato paste

2 tablespoons minced canned chipotle peppers in adobo sauce

1¼ cups grated Monterey Jack, or pepper Jack cheese

½ cup reduced-fat sour cream

½ cup fresh cilantro, chopped

2 limes, cut into wedges

 

Instructions:

1. Soak beans overnight in 2 quarts water. (Alternatively, place beans and 2 quarts water in a large pot. Bring to a boil. Boil for 2 minutes. Remove from heat and let stand for 1 hour). Drain the beans, discarding soaking liquid.

2. Combine oil, mustard seeds, chili powder, cumin and cardamom in a 5- to 6-quart Dutch oven. Place over high heat and stir until the spices sizzle, about 30 seconds. Add onions, mushrooms (ONLY IF USING FRESH – ADD DRIED MUSHROOMS IN STEP 3), tomatillos and water. Cover and cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are juicy, 5 to 7 minutes. Uncover and stir often until the juices evaporate and the vegetables are lightly browned, 10 to 15 minutes. Add broth, tomato paste and chipotles; mix well.

3. Place the beans in a 5- to 6-quart slow cooker. Pour the hot vegetable mixture over the beans. Turn heat to high. Put the lid on and cook until the beans are creamy, 5 to 8 hours. (NOTE: I USED THE STOVETOP OPTION – SEE BELOW).

4. Garnish each serving with cheese, a dollop of sour cream and a sprinkling of cilantro. Serve with lime wedges.

 

Stovetop Variation: Total: 4 1/2 hours In Step 2, increase broth to 81/2 cups. Omit Step 3. Add the beans to the Dutch oven; cover and simmer the chili gently over low heat, stirring occasionally, until the beans are creamy to bite, about 3 hours. PS - I DIDN'T USE A DUTCH OVEN, JUST A LARGE HEAVY SOUP POT.
