Ratatouille

Recipe courtesy of French Women Don't Get Fat Cookbook, 2004

1½ lbs roma tomatoes

1½ lbs squash (half zucchini and half crooked neck)

1½ lbs eggplant

12 cloves garlic, minced

1 large bunch of basil

Olive oil

Salt & fresh ground pepper

 

Wash and peel the eggplant and cut into thin slices (between ⅛" & ⅝" thick). Place the some of the eggplant slices on a single layer on a couple paper towels. When the layer is full, cover with another couple paper towels and repeat. Once all of the eggplant slices are layered between paper towels, cover the top with a layer of towels and a cutting board with weight on it to press the acid out of the eggplant. Let set for 30 minutes.

 

Wash and slice the squash and tomatoes into thin slices (between ⅛" & ⅝" thick), a Mandoline works well for this. Keep each vegetable in separate bowls and save that juice from the tomatoes.

 

Lightly oil the bottom and sides of a heavy stock pot.

 

Layer the vegetables in layers, starting with the eggplant. Push down the vegetables about ½ of the way through and then when done layering

  • Eggplant
  • Tomatoes
  • Squash (alternating yellow and green)
  • Herbs
  • Salt and pepper

 

.Once complete, add the juice from the tomatoes (gently pour over the top).

 

Cover and cook at 300° for 2½ hours.

 

Slice and serve. Salt and pepper to taste.

 

Add a little fresh thyme to the top (be careful not to add too much)

 

Garnish with a good olive oil, Parmesan cheese and herbs.

Serve with warm bread.

 

Serves 6
