Smoked Salmon

Recipe courtesy of Darren Kessler (from Mitchel), 2017

3 lb salmon filet, no skin

2 Cups Dark Brown Sugar

½ cup Kosher Salt

1-2 tbls fresh ground pepper

4-5 rough chopped garlic cloves

1 jar of Apricot jam or honey.

 

Cure

2 Cups Dark Brown Sugar

½ cup Mortons Kosher Salt

1-2 tbls fresh ground pepper

4-5 rough chopped garlic cloves

Mix together the cure ingredients in a bowl.

 

Cut salmon into roughly 2 inch wide portions.

Put down a layer of cure into a glass or plastic dish (not metal).

Layer in salmon portions and cover with more cure.

Add second layer of salmon portions as needed and cover with cure.

Cover salmon with a layer of saran wrap tightly to seal out some air. Cover the glass dish with another piece of saran wrap.

Place in the refrigerator over night (at least 8 hours. 24 is better.)

 

Drying

After curing in the refer overnight remove from fridge and rinse thoroughly each piece with cold water. Lay the rinsed pieces onto a greased or sprayed or olive oiled rack. Pat dry each portion with a paper towel.

Allow the salmon portions to sit on the rack for a couple of hours. Inside in the house is fine. Some people even say to blow a fan on them during this time. You want the pieces to develop a skin coating. Should be shiny after a couple of hours.

 

Smoking

Place the rack of salmon portions on the smoker. I try to keep the smoker around 210-225. Smoke for about an hour and a quarter. At that point brush on melted apricot jam or honey and smoke for another 30 minutes.

 

Serve with cream cheese on crackers with green onion or other herbs.
