Mediterranean Sole

Recipe courtesy of The Mediterranean Dish - January, 2015

Sauce:

1 fresh lime juice

½ cup extra virgin olive oil

½ cup unsalted melted butter

2 shallots, thinly sliced

3 garlic cloves, thinly-sliced

2 tbsp capers

 

Rub:

I tsp seasoned salt, or to your taste

¾ tsp ground black pepper

1 tsp ground cumin

1 tsp garlic powder

 

Fish and toppings:

1.5 lb Sole fillet (about 10-12 thin fillets)

4-6 green onions, top trimmed, halved length-wise

1 lime slices (thin)

¾ cup roughly chopped fresh dill for garnish

 

In a small bowl, whisk together lime juice, olive oil and melted butter with a dash of seasoned salt. Stir in the shallots, garlic and capers. Note: The sauce can be made a day in advance and refrigerated.

 

In a separate small bowl, mix together the seasoned salt, pepper, cumin and garlic powder. Spice fish fillets each on both sides.

 

Preheat the grill or oven to ~375 degrees.

 

Add a thin coat of the butter lime mixture on a lightly-oiled comal (or large baking pan/dish). Place fish on the pan and cover it with the buttery lime mixture. Arrange green onion halves and lime slices on top of the fish fillets.

 

Cook for 10 - 15 minutes in a hot grill. Do not over cook.

 

 

Remove from grill or oven and garnish with fresh dill.

 

Server as a tapas right on the comal.

 

Serve next to white rice or roasted Greek potatoes
