Black-Eyed Pea New Years Soup

Recipe courtesy of Michela, 2014

Ingredients

1 pound dried black-eyed peas

2 tablespoons olive oil

½- ¾ package MorningStar Farms Veggie Bacon Strips (cooked and chopped)

½- ¾ package Sweet Earth Hickory & Sage Bacon (cooked and chopped)

1 medium onion, diced

4 garlic cloves, diced

1½ teaspoons salt

1 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper

½ teaspoon cayenne pepper

1 teaspoon garlic powder

4 cups weak vegetarian chicken stock (better than bullion) - make it weak (maybe one tsp)

2 cups water

2-3 dried ancho chiles

1 oz. package of dried shiitake mushrooms (halved and sliced)

 

3 bay leaves

 

Put dried black-eyed peas in a large pot and cover with about 4 inches of water. Soak the peas overnight, then drain the water and rinse the peas.

 

Cook the bacon strips and set aside on a paper towel to soak up any excess oil.

 

Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat. When the oil is shimmering, add the onion and garlic and cook until lightly browned, about 6 to 8 minutes. Add the salt, black pepper, cayenne and garlic powder and chopped up 'bacon'. Cook until the entire mixture is coated with the spices, about 2 minutes.

 

Pour in the stock and water into a crock pot and drop in the bay leaves and ancho chili. Set on auto and cook covered, for about a few hours, until the peas are soft.

 

Using the back of a spoon, smash some of the peas against the inside of the pot then stir them into the mixture. This will break up some of the peas and give them a creamier consistency.

 

Serve over rice.

 

Taste for seasonings, and add some hot-pepper vinegar, if desired. Discard the bay leaves and transfer the black-eyed peas to a serving bowl.

 

Yield:6 to 8 servings
