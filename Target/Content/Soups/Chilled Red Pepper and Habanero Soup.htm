Chilled Red Pepper and Habanero Soup

Recipe adapted from of Epicurious, 2008 by Frank and Michela

4 medium red bell peppers

2 pounds tomatoes (vine ripened)

1 large sweet onion, chopped

3 garlic cloves, chopped

3 to 4 fresh habanero chiles, finely minced (2 to 3 tablespoons), stems and seeds discarded

¼ cup plus 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided

1¾ cups vegetarian chicken broth*

Fresh cilantro, chopped for garnish

Smoked Gouda Cheese Crips (see page 1)

Habanero Bread Crisps (see page 1)

 

Grill the peppers and tomatoes until the skins are slightly charred and easy to peel off. Let cool for 10 minutes so that they are not too hot to handle. Peel the tomatoes and coarsely chop, reserving the liquid. Peel, stem and seed the peppers then coarsely chop, reserving any liquid.

 

Cook onion, garlic, chiles, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1/4 teaspoon pepper in 2 tablespoons oil in a 3- to 4-quart heavy saucepan over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until softened and pale golden, about 8 minutes. Add tomatoes with juices, bell peppers, broth, and 1/4 teaspoon salt and simmer, covered, until peppers are tender, about 5 minutes.

 

Purée the soup using a submersion blender or in 2 or 3 batches in a regular blender (use caution when blending hot liquids), drizzling remaining 1/4 cup oil while blending. Quick-chill soup in a metal bowl set in an ice bath, stirring often, 10 to 15 minutes. Season with salt as needed.

 

Serve and garnish with cheese crisps and cilantro.

 

*For the 'chicken broth', we use "Better than Bouillon" No Chicken Base and start with 1/2 tsp, adding more to taste before blending the soup.

 

Serve with Smoked Gouda Cheese Crips (see page 1) and Habanero Bread Crisps (see page 1)

 

We added chopped Avocado into the center cheese crisp, it was also suggested to add Crème Fraîche.

 

Soup can be made 3 days ahead and chilled.
